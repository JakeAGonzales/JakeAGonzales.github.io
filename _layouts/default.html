I’m currently a PhD student at the University of Washington where I am advised by Prof. Behcet Acikmese and Prof. Lillian Ratliff. My research interests are broadly at the intersection of control theory, machine learning, and optimization. I’m currently working on developing safe and scalable decision-making algorithms for multi-agent autonomous mobility.

Previously, I got an undergraduate degree in electrical engineering from the University of New Mexico. There, I worked in the Human-Centered Systems and Control Lab where I was advised by Prof. Meeko Oishi. I did research in data-driven stochastic optimal control.
